Welcome to the Persian Digital Library Corpus

Welcome to the Persian Digital Library (PDL) at the university of Maryland Corpus website. For more info on the PDL visit the Roshan Initiative in Persian Digital Humanities at the University of Maryland PersDig@UMD.

At University of Marylandâ€™s Roshan Institute for Persian Studies, our Persian digital humanities team wants to shepherd the textual tradition of Persian culture into the digital age by creating the Persian Digital Library and radically transforming the ways Persian language, literature and culture are learned, studied and preserved in the 21st century.

What did the Persian Digital Library (PDL) Pilot Project do?

  • created an open-access corpus of more than 60,000 Persian poems from the raw texts provided by Ganjoor, formatted according to the CapiTainS canonical text services (CTS) standards.
  • validated PDL texts with the CapiTainS Tool Suite of Perseus Digital Library and deposited them in their CTS repository.

Note: All texts, programs, and technology developed during this project will be open access/open source and will be made publicly available for everyone in the world to download, use, reuse, and adapt.
